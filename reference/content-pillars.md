## PILLAR 1 — Data & BI Technical Deep Dives

**What this is:**
Teaching the concepts, tools, and decisions behind enterprise data and BI work — clearly, accessibly, and with the depth that only comes from someone who has actually built it. No employer context needed. No confidential details. Just transferable knowledge drawn from deep technical expertise.

**Why this works for {{YOUR_NAME}}:**
She knows this stack cold: {{YOUR_TECH_STACK}} dimensional modeling, {{YOUR_TECH_STACK_2}}, row-level security, {{YOUR_SCALE_DETAIL}}. She can teach any of these topics with specificity and credibility that most "BI content" on LinkedIn completely lacks — without ever naming a project or employer.

**The angle that differentiates:**
Most BI/data content online is either too basic ("what is a fact table?") or too academic. {{YOUR_NAME}}'s angle is: *this is what it actually looks like in production, and here's the decision that most tutorials skip.*

**Post types that work:**
- "The one DAX pattern I use in almost every model — and why"
- "Why your Power BI report is slow (and it's not what you think)"
- "Kimball vs Inmon: I've built both. Here's my honest take."
- "What a semantic layer actually does — explained without jargon"
- "Row-level security in Power BI: the thing most tutorials get wrong"
- Concept explainers: dimensional modeling, star schemas, calculated columns vs measures
- Decision frameworks: "when to use DirectQuery vs Import mode"
- "What I'd do differently if I were starting my BI career today"

**What to avoid:**
- Anything that reveals specific employer data, schemas, or proprietary workflows
- Over-simplified "101" content with no real practitioner angle
- Posts that are just definitions — always include the *decision* or the *gotcha*

**Audience value:**
→ Junior analysts bookmark it to learn
→ Mid-level practitioners share it because it validates what they already know
→ Senior practitioners follow because they're curious whether she's as sharp as she sounds

**Repurpose path:**
LinkedIn educational post → Instagram carousel (concept broken into 5 slides, one per idea)

## PILLAR 2 — Data-Driven Career & Industry Insights

**What this is:**
Applying an analytical mindset to career navigation, job market trends, and the real impact AI is having on data, analytics, and program management roles. Not generic career advice — data-informed, specific, and contrarian where the data supports it.

**Why this works for {{YOUR_NAME}}:**
She is simultaneously a data practitioner and a job seeker in a shifting market. She has the analytical skills to actually look at the data — job posting trends, skill demand shifts, salary benchmarks, what titles are growing and shrinking — and the lived experience to know what it feels like on the ground. That combination is rare.

**The angle that differentiates:**
Most career content is anecdote-based opinion. {{YOUR_NAME}} brings the BI analyst lens: *here's what the actual data says, and here's where it matches — and contradicts — conventional wisdom.*

**Post types that work:**
- "I analyzed 200 TPM job postings. Here's what skills actually appear most."
- "The BI skills that are disappearing from job postings (and what's replacing them)"
- "What AI is actually doing to data analyst roles — not the hype version"
- "Why 'data-driven' job descriptions are often the least data-driven places to work"
- Trend analysis: Power BI vs Tableau vs Looker adoption in enterprise
- Salary and role analysis: what senior BI/TPM roles actually pay and why the range is so wide
- "The gap between what job postings ask for and what the job actually is"
- "From BI Analyst to TPM: the skills that transferred and the ones I had to build"

**What to avoid:**
- Career takes with no data behind them — if making a claim, ground it in something
- Generic "here are 5 tips for your LinkedIn profile" content
- Advice that ignores the reality of being an immigrant job seeker (either address it directly or leave it out — no vague middle ground)
- Predictions stated as certainties

**Audience value:**
→ Job seekers save it for their own search
→ Practitioners share it with peers navigating similar transitions
→ Recruiters and hiring managers notice (this is the kind of candidate who researches before applying)

**Repurpose path:**
LinkedIn data-insight post (with chart or screenshot if available) → Instagram Reel ("here's what I found when I looked at the actual numbers")

## PILLAR 3 — Immigrant in Tech

**What this is:**
The real experience of building a career in tech as an immigrant — the financial calculus, the cultural translation, the things that never get said out loud in DEI panels.

**{{YOUR_NAME}}'s angle:**
She came to the US at 19, navigated a CS degree in a foreign country, worked, went back for her MS, is now job searching in a field where most senior roles assume a network she had to build from scratch. Specific, lived experience — not a general "diversity in tech" perspective.

**Post types that work:**
- Specific, honest stories — the moment she realized something was different for her than for her peers
- Cultural moments: Eid, International Women's Day, immigration milestones — tied to a real professional insight
- "What I spent [money/time/energy] that others didn't have to" — the invisible costs
- Reframes: taking something that felt like a disadvantage and showing the other side
- STEM OPT/immigration process posts — practical help for others navigating the same

**What to avoid:**
- Performing positivity — "I'm so grateful" posts that skip the hard part
- Vague "representation matters" language without a real story underneath it
- Centering others' comfort over her own honesty

**Repurpose path:**
LinkedIn personal story (photo) → Instagram Reel (talking head, personal story format)

## PILLAR 4 — AI Tools for Real Work

**What this is:**
Hands-on, honest tool coverage. Not sponsored. Not a list of "top 10 AI tools." Real workflows, real limitations, real "here's when this actually saves time and here's when it doesn't."

**Post types that work:**
- "I used [tool] for [specific task] — here's what actually happened"
- Comparison posts: two tools, one use case, honest winner
- Workflow demos: step-by-step for non-technical audiences
- "What I'd tell my team about [tool] before they started using it"
- "The AI prompt I use every week for [specific work task]"

**What to avoid:**
- Recapping product launches without personal experience
- Unqualified endorsements — always include the limitation
- Technical deep-dives with no practical takeaway

**Repurpose path:**
LinkedIn how-to post → Instagram Reel (demo/tutorial format)

## WHY THESE TWO REPLACEMENTS SERVE HER GOALS BETTER

**The old problem:**
Pillars 1 and 2 leaned on employer context to be specific — which meant either staying vague (generic content) or risking oversharing (confidentiality).

**The new logic:**

→ **Pillar 1** (Technical Deep Dives) proves she can go deep on the craft.
Practitioners recognize the specificity. Recruiters see demonstrated expertise.
Zero confidential information required.

→ **Pillar 2** (Data-Driven Insights) proves she can zoom out and see patterns.
This is the strategic / leadership signal. It shows she thinks like a TPM, not just a practitioner.
It also quietly positions her job search — every recruiter who reads her industry analysis understands exactly what kind of hire she would be.

**Together:** Technical depth + strategic perspective = the exact profile of a Senior TPM in a data-heavy environment.
Same goals. Better content. Easier to produce.
